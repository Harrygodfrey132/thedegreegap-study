Why Cambridge families choose The Degree Gap

Cambridge is an unusual place to grow up. The academic expectations that come with being surrounded by one of the world's great universities filter down into secondary education in ways that students elsewhere rarely experience. Entry to Hills Road Sixth Form is competitive, the gap between a strong grade and a top grade matters more at this level than almost anywhere else, and many students here are already working hard. Online tuition that keeps Cambridge students moving toward their goals

All tutoring with Cambridge students is delivered online through Lessonspace, a platform built for focused one-to-one sessions. Students and tutors work together on a shared interactive whiteboard, which makes it possible to tackle problem sets, annotate texts, and work through complex questions in real time rather than just watching a screen.

When something makes sense, the tutor moves forward. There is no waiting for a class to catch up.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Do you help with sixth form entrance or university application preparation?

Yes. We support Cambridge students preparing for competitive sixth form entry, including Hills Road and Long Road, as well as students building towards strong UCAS applications. Tutors understand what selective institutions are looking for and can prepare students accordingly.

Can tutors help students who are already achieving highly but want to go further?

Absolutely. Many of our Cambridge students are already strong performers who want to push towards the top of the grade boundary or develop the deeper subject understanding that Oxbridge and Russell Group applications require. Our tutors are well suited to that kind of stretch work.

What qualifications do your tutors have?

Tutors working with Cambridge students are graduates from strong UK universities, many from Russell Group institutions. All are individually vetted for subject knowledge, teaching ability and communication before working with students. Several have first-hand experience of Cambridge itself.

How quickly can we get started?

Once we understand what the student needs, we aim to suggest a suitable tutor quickly. We handle the matching so families are not left searching through profiles. For Cambridge students with upcoming assessments or application deadlines, we prioritise getting a strong match in place as soon as possible.
