Supporting Oxford Students at Every Stage

Oxford has no grammar schools — every state secondary is comprehensive, which means entry to the strongest sixth forms is decided entirely by GCSE results. Cheney School placed in the top ten per cent of state schools nationally for student progress in 2024, a sign that Oxford's best comprehensives set a high bar. For a student aiming at one of those sixth forms, or at the independent options like Magdalen College School or Oxford High School GDST, a handful of grade 6s is rarely enough. They need results that make an application stand out.

The Degree Gap works with Oxford students who want to reach that standard. Our tutors do not repeat lessons — they identify the specific gaps and weaknesses that are holding a grade back and target those precisely. Whether a student needs a GCSE tutor to secure the results that open the right doors at sixteen, or an A-Level tutor to push predicted grades to the level a competitive university requires, we match them with someone who knows the subject and what the highest marks actually need.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Do you help with sixth form entrance exams or interviews?

Yes. We support Oxford students preparing for competitive sixth form entry, including aptitude tests, subject-specific assessments and interview preparation. Tutors understand what selective schools and sixth form colleges are looking for and can prepare students accordingly.

Is there a free consultation before we commit?

Yes. We start with a conversation to understand what the student needs before recommending anyone. There is no pressure and no obligation. We would rather take the time to get the match right for your Oxford student than rush an introduction.

What qualifications do your tutors have?

Tutors working with Oxford students are graduates from strong UK universities, many from Russell Group institutions. All have been individually vetted for subject knowledge, teaching ability and communication before working with students.

How long does it take to see an improvement?

Most Oxford students notice a difference within the first few sessions, not necessarily in grades straight away, but in how they approach the subject and how much clearer the harder questions feel. Grade improvements typically follow within a half term of consistent sessions.
