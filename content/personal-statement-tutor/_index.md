A personal statement is the only part of your UCAS application where admissions tutors hear your voice directly. It is also the part most applicants get wrong: not because they lack the experience, but because they don't know what a particular department actually wants to read.

Our tutors work subject by subject because the criteria genuinely differ. What makes a strong medicine personal statement has very little overlap with what makes a strong computer science one. The tone, the structure, the balance between academic interest and practical experience: all of it varies. Generic advice leads to generic applications.

Choose your subject below for tailored guidance on what to write, what to avoid, and how to present your application in the strongest possible light.
