WHEN TO START

At Every Stage of GCSE

Students come to us at different points in their GCSE journey. Here's what each stage looks like — and what one-to-one tutoring focuses on.

Year 9

Choosing Options

Year 9 is earlier than most families expect — but students starting now build a real head start. Tutoring at this stage focuses on securing foundations in Maths and English before GCSE content begins, and on subject choices that match where a student's strengths actually are.

Foundation-building & subject confidence
Year 10

The Quiet Slide

Year 10 is when content volume increases and gaps from earlier years start to show. This is the most common entry point for students we work with. Tutoring focuses on identifying exactly where understanding broke down — and fixing it properly, not covering it over.

Gap diagnosis & content catch-up
Year 11

Exam Preparation

With mocks and finals in sight, Year 11 tutoring becomes highly targeted. Tutors work through past papers, sharpen exam technique, and build the calm confidence that turns preparation into performance on the day. It's not about learning more — it's about using what's there.

EXAM BOARD EXPERTISE

Matched to Your Exact Specification

The same subject can look entirely different across exam boards. A tutor matched to your child's specific board knows the mark scheme, the question styles, and the traps. That specificity is the difference between preparing and preparing well.

When you get in touch, we'll ask about your child's school and exam board before making any introduction. The tutor we recommend will know their specific specification.

Get Matched
AQA

The most widely used GCSE board in England. AQA exams reward structured written responses and precise use of terminology. Tutors matched to AQA target the exact content and mark scheme for every unit.

Edexcel

Known for numerical precision in Maths and Sciences, and for longer analytical essay questions in Humanities. Edexcel tutors understand how assessment objectives map to the mark scheme.

OCR

OCR specifications often include a higher proportion of application-based questions. Students who understand the context behind the content consistently outperform those who rely on memorisation.

WJEC

Common in Wales and used in a number of English schools. WJEC tutors understand where the specification diverges from other boards and prepare students for the exact questions they'll face.
